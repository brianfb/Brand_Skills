# Brand Audit Rubric

This rubric provides scoring criteria for evaluating existing brand materials against the Brand DNA framework. Use this in Brand Audit Mode to identify gaps, inconsistencies, and opportunities.

---

## Audit Overview

**Purpose:** Evaluate the health and completeness of an existing brand's strategic foundation.

**Output:** A scored audit report with gap analysis and prioritized recommendations.

**Process:**
1. Gather existing brand materials
2. Score each of 11 framework components
3. Calculate overall brand health score
4. Identify critical gaps and quick wins
5. Recommend next steps (mini-workshops for weak areas)

---

## Scoring Scale

Each component is scored 1-5:

| Score | Label | Definition |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **5** | Exceptional | Clearly defined, distinctive, consistently applied, and battle-tested |
| **4** | Strong | Well-defined and mostly consistent, minor refinement opportunities |
| **3** | Adequate | Exists but generic, inconsistently applied, or needs sharpening |
| **2** | Weak | Vague, contradictory, or missing key elements |
| **1** | Missing | Not defined or completely ineffective |

---

## Component Scoring Criteria

### 1. Purpose (Weight: High)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is there a stated reason for existing beyond profit?
- Does it articulate the impact or change the brand wants to make?
- Is it specific enough to guide decisions?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Purpose is unique, inspiring, and clearly drives strategic decisions. Could not apply to competitors.
- **4:** Purpose is clear and meaningful but could be slightly sharper or more distinctive.
- **3:** Purpose exists but is generic ("make the world better") or functional ("provide great products").
- **2:** Purpose is vague, confused with mission, or not actionable.
- **1:** No stated purpose or purpose is purely profit-focused.

**Audit questions:**
- Can employees articulate the purpose without reading it?
- Has the purpose ever influenced a difficult decision?
- Would a competitor's purpose be interchangeable with this one?

---

### 2. Vision (Weight: High)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is there a future state the brand is working toward?
- Is it aspirational enough to inspire but concrete enough to measure progress?
- Does it describe impact, not market share?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Vision describes a compelling future that people would fight for. Specific and inspiring.
- **4:** Vision is clear and aspirational but could be more vivid or distinctive.
- **3:** Vision exists but focuses on business goals ("be #1") rather than impact.
- **2:** Vision is too vague to inspire action or guide strategy.
- **1:** No stated vision.

**Audit questions:**
- Does the vision describe the world, not just the company?
- Could the team explain how today's work connects to this vision?
- Is this vision different from "industry standard" vision statements?

---

### 3. Mission (Weight: High)

**What to evaluate:**
- Does it state what the brand does, for whom, and how?
- Is it present-tense and actionable?
- Does it provide clear direction for daily work?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Mission is specific, actionable, and clearly guides what the brand does and doesn't do.
- **4:** Mission is clear but could be more specific about audience or approach.
- **3:** Mission exists but is abstract ("deliver excellence") or lists activities without impact.
- **2:** Mission is confused with purpose/vision or too vague to guide decisions.
- **1:** No stated mission.

**Audit questions:**
- Does the mission help prioritize features or initiatives?
- Is the target audience specific enough to identify?
- Could a new employee use this to understand their role?

---

### 4. Core Values (Weight: High)

**What to evaluate:**
- Are there 3-5 clearly stated values?
- Are they specific enough to create real constraints?
- Have they ever cost the company something?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Values are distinctive, create real trade-offs, and visibly guide decisions. Each has clear definition.
- **4:** Values are clear and mostly distinctive but 1-2 could be sharper.
- **3:** Values exist but are generic (integrity, excellence, innovation) or not applied consistently.
- **2:** Values are stated but contradictory, too numerous, or clearly aspirational rather than real.
- **1:** No stated values or values are meaningless corporate speak.

**Audit questions:**
- Has a value ever led to firing someone or rejecting revenue?
- Would any company explicitly disagree with these values?
- Can employees recite values without looking them up?

---

### 5. Brand Personality (Weight: Medium)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is the brand's human character defined?
- Are personality traits specific and distinctive?
- Is personality consistently expressed across touchpoints?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Personality is vivid, distinctive, and consistently embodied. Clear archetype or trait set.
- **4:** Personality is defined and mostly consistent but could be more specific.
- **3:** Personality exists but uses generic traits (professional, friendly) or is inconsistently applied.
- **2:** Personality is vague, contradictory, or undefined.
- **1:** No defined personality.

**Audit questions:**
- Could someone describe the brand as if describing a person?
- Is the personality different from the top 3 competitors?
- Do all touchpoints (website, social, support) feel like the same "person"?

---

### 6. Brand Voice & Tone (Weight: High)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is voice defined with specific dimensions (pace, vocabulary, rhetoric)?
- Is tone mapped to different contexts?
- Are there clear examples and guidelines?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Voice is clearly defined with examples, tone mapping, and do's/don'ts. Consistently applied.
- **4:** Voice is well-defined but missing examples or tone mapping for some contexts.
- **3:** Voice is described generally but lacks specificity or consistent application.
- **2:** Voice is vague ("professional and friendly") with no actionable guidance.
- **1:** No defined voice.

**Audit questions:**
- Could a new writer produce on-brand copy from the voice guidelines?
- Is there a clear difference between voice (constant) and tone (contextual)?
- Does the error message sound like the marketing headline?

---

### 7. Positioning (Weight: High)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is target audience clearly defined (demographics + psychographics)?
- Is there a clear category/competitive frame?
- Is differentiation specific and defensible?
- Is there a positioning statement?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Positioning is analytically precise with clear audience, category, differentiation, and statement.
- **4:** Positioning is strong but one element (audience, differentiation, or statement) could be sharper.
- **3:** Positioning exists but is generic, unclear on differentiation, or missing the "only" test.
- **2:** Positioning is vague or based on claims competitors could also make.
- **1:** No defined positioning.

**Audit questions:**
- Could someone explain why to choose this brand over the top alternative?
- Is the target audience specific enough to exclude people?
- Does the positioning statement pass the "only" test?

---

### 8. Brand Story (Weight: Medium)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is there a narrative arc (not just company history)?
- Does it include tension, struggle, and breakthrough?
- Is it emotionally resonant and memorable?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Story is compelling, follows narrative structure, and creates emotional connection.
- **4:** Story is clear and engaging but could be more specific or emotionally resonant.
- **3:** Story exists but reads like company history rather than narrative.
- **2:** Story is generic, missing key elements, or unmemorable.
- **1:** No brand story.

**Audit questions:**
- Is there an "inciting moment" that makes the story unique?
- Does the story make the customer the hero?
- Would someone remember this story after hearing it once?

---

### 9. Visual Identity Direction (Weight: Medium)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is there strategic direction for color, typography, and imagery?
- Is the visual direction distinctive and aligned with personality?
- Is it clear enough to guide creative work?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Visual direction is distinctive, clearly defined, and consistently executed.
- **4:** Visual direction is clear but could be more specific or distinctive.
- **3:** Visual direction exists but is generic or inconsistently applied.
- **2:** Visual direction is vague or contradictory.
- **1:** No defined visual direction.

**Audit questions:**
- Could a designer create on-brand work from this direction alone?
- Is the visual feel different from top competitors?
- Does the visual direction align with the brand personality?

---

### 10. Tagline / Mantra (Weight: Low)

**What to evaluate:**
- Is there a memorable tagline or internal mantra?
- Does it capture the brand's essence?
- Is it distinctive and ownable?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Tagline is memorable, distinctive, and perfectly captures the brand essence.
- **4:** Tagline is good but could be sharper or more memorable.
- **3:** Tagline exists but is generic or doesn't fully capture the brand.
- **2:** Tagline is forgettable or could apply to any competitor.
- **1:** No tagline.

**Audit questions:**
- Would someone remember this a week after hearing it?
- Does the tagline pass the competitor test (could they use it)?
- Does it align with the purpose and positioning?

---

### 11. Brand Do's and Don'ts (Weight: Medium)

**What to evaluate:**
- Are there concrete behavioral guidelines?
- Do they cover communication, visual, and behavioral categories?
- Are they specific and actionable?

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Guidelines are comprehensive, specific, and actively used to guide decisions.
- **4:** Guidelines exist and are mostly clear but missing some categories.
- **3:** Guidelines exist but are too general or not actively referenced.
- **2:** Guidelines are vague or incomplete.
- **1:** No guidelines.

**Audit questions:**
- Could someone use these guidelines to make a quick decision?
- Are there specific prohibitions, not just positive statements?
- Do guidelines cover edge cases and difficult situations?

---

## Calculating Brand Health Score

**Weighted scoring:**

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Purpose | 1.5x |
| Vision | 1.5x |
| Mission | 1.5x |
| Core Values | 1.5x |
| Brand Personality | 1.0x |
| Brand Voice & Tone | 1.5x |
| Positioning | 1.5x |
| Brand Story | 1.0x |
| Visual Identity Direction | 1.0x |
| Tagline / Mantra | 0.5x |
| Brand Do's and Don'ts | 1.0x |

**Total possible weighted score:** 62.5 points

**Health rating:**

| Score Range | Rating | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| 56-62.5 | Exceptional | Brand DNA is comprehensive and distinctive |
| 44-55 | Strong | Solid foundation with minor refinement opportunities |
| 31-43 | Developing | Core elements in place but significant gaps exist |
| 19-30 | Weak | Major gaps requiring strategic work |
| 0-18 | Critical | Brand DNA needs fundamental development |

## Materials to Request for Audit

When initiating a brand audit, request:

1. **Existing brand documents:**
   - Brand guidelines or style guide
   - Mission/vision/values statements
   - Positioning documents or brand strategy decks

2. **Communication samples:**
   - Website homepage and about page
   - Recent social media posts (5-10)
   - Marketing emails or campaigns
   - Product copy or UI text

3. **Visual materials:**
   - Logo and color palette
   - Recent design work (ads, social graphics)
   - Photography or illustration samples

4. **Internal documents (if available):**
   - Culture documents
   - Hiring or onboarding materials
   - Internal communications
