# Brand DNA Presentation Template

This template defines the slide structure for generating a stakeholder presentation from a completed Brand DNA document.

---

## Presentation Overview

**Purpose:** Communicate the brand strategy to stakeholders, team members, investors, or partners in a visually engaging format.

**Format:** 10-12 slides, designed for cross-skill invocation with `document-skills:pptx`

**Duration:** 15-20 minute presentation

---

## Slide-by-Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Title Slide

**Content:**
- Brand name (large, centered)
- Tagline (primary option from Brand DNA)
- Subtitle: "Brand DNA" or "Brand Strategy"
- Date and presenter name (optional)

**Speaker Notes:**
"This presentation captures the strategic foundation of [Brand Name] — who we are, what we stand for, and how we show up in the world. This is the DNA that should inform every decision we make."

**Design Direction:**
- Clean, minimal layout
- Brand name should dominate
- Use brand's visual direction for color/typography feel

---

### Slide 2: The Problem

**Content:**
- Headline: "The Problem We Exist to Solve" or "Why We're Here"
- 2-3 bullet points describing audience pain points
- Pull from: Audience & Problem section, positioning target audience frustrations

**Speaker Notes:**
"Before we talk about who we are, let's talk about the problem we're solving. Our audience is dealing with [summarize key pain points]. Existing solutions fall short because [gap]."

**Design Direction:**
- Bold, attention-grabbing headline
- Consider a "before" visual metaphor
- Emphasize tension and frustration

---

### Slide 3: Purpose

**Content:**
- Headline: "Why We Exist"
- Purpose statement (1-2 sentences, large text)
- Optional: Supporting context or "what the world would lose" statement

**Speaker Notes:**
"Our purpose is the reason we exist beyond profit. If we disappeared tomorrow, [what would be lost]. This purpose guides every strategic decision."

**Design Direction:**
- Purpose statement should be the visual focus
- Minimal supporting text
- Aspirational, inspiring tone

---

### Slide 4: Vision & Mission

**Content:**
- Split layout or stacked:
  - **Vision** (top): "The future we're building toward"
  - **Mission** (bottom): "What we do today to get there"
- Vision statement (aspirational future)
- Mission statement (present-tense action)

**Speaker Notes:**
"Our vision is the future we're working toward — [vision]. Our mission is how we're getting there today — [mission]. Vision inspires. Mission directs."

**Design Direction:**
- Clear visual distinction between vision and mission
- Vision = expansive, aspirational
- Mission = grounded, actionable

---

### Slide 5: Core Values

**Content:**
- Headline: "What We Stand For" or "Our Core Values"
- 3-5 values with names and brief descriptions
- Each value should have a name + 1-line explanation

**Speaker Notes:**
"These aren't aspirational platitudes — they're the principles that guide hard decisions. Each value should occasionally cost us something. [Give an example of a value in action]."

**Design Direction:**
- Values as distinct visual elements (cards, columns, or list)
- Value names should be bold/prominent
- Descriptions secondary

---

### Slide 6: Personality

**Content:**
- Headline: "Who We Are" or "Our Brand Personality"
- Primary archetype (if used)
- 3-5 personality adjectives
- The "dinner party" description or personality summary
- Optional: trait spectrum visualization

**Speaker Notes:**
"If our brand were a person at a party, they'd be [description]. We're [adjectives]. This personality should come through in every touchpoint."

**Design Direction:**
- Human, approachable feel
- Consider icons or illustrations for archetypes
- Personality adjectives as visual callouts

---

### Slide 7: Voice

**Content:**
- Headline: "How We Sound" or "Our Brand Voice"
- Voice characteristics (3-4 key dimensions)
- 2-3 example sentences showing voice in action
- Optional: Voice Do's and Don'ts as a quick reference

**Speaker Notes:**
"Voice is how we speak — it should be consistent everywhere. Our voice is [characteristics]. Here's what that sounds like in practice: [read examples]."

**Design Direction:**
- Include actual copy examples
- Consider a "sounds like / doesn't sound like" comparison
- Typography should reflect the voice it's describing

---

### Slide 8: Positioning

**Content:**
- Headline: "Where We Stand" or "Our Position"
- Target audience (1-2 sentences)
- Category / competitive frame
- Differentiation (what makes us the only ___)
- Positioning statement (formatted clearly)

**Speaker Notes:**
"Positioning is about the space we occupy in our audience's mind. For [audience], we're the [category] that [benefit] because [reason to believe]. Our competitors can't claim this because [differentiation]."

**Design Direction:**
- Positioning statement should be prominent
- Consider a simple competitive landscape visual
- Clear hierarchy: audience → category → differentiation → statement

---

### Slide 9: Story

**Content:**
- Headline: "Our Story" or "The Journey"
- Narrative arc condensed:
  - The problem/frustration that started it all
  - The breakthrough moment
  - Where we're headed
- Can be bullet points or short paragraphs

**Speaker Notes:**
"Every brand needs a story that makes it memorable. Ours begins with [origin tension], led to [breakthrough], and is now heading toward [vision]. This story is what makes us human."

**Design Direction:**
- Narrative flow (timeline, story arc visual, or sequential layout)
- Emotional, human feel
- Consider a founder photo or origin imagery

---

### Slide 10: Visual Direction

**Content:**
- Headline: "How We Look" or "Visual Identity Direction"
- Color mood description
- Typography feel
- Imagery style
- Overall aesthetic summary
- Optional: Reference brands or mood board description

**Speaker Notes:**
"This isn't a design spec — it's strategic direction. Our visual world should feel [overall aesthetic]. Think [reference brands] but [how we differ]."

**Design Direction:**
- This slide should *embody* the visual direction it describes
- Consider color swatches or mood descriptors
- Typography on this slide should reflect the brand's type feel

---

### Slide 11: Do's & Don'ts

**Content:**
- Headline: "Brand Guardrails" or "Do's & Don'ts"
- Two columns:
  - **Do:** 4-6 concrete guidelines
  - **Don't:** 4-6 specific prohibitions
- Pull from communication, visual, behavioral categories

**Speaker Notes:**
"These guardrails help anyone representing the brand make quick decisions. When in doubt, check these rules. [Highlight 1-2 specific examples]."

**Design Direction:**
- Clear two-column layout
- Checkmarks / X marks for visual scanning
- Actionable, specific items

---

### Slide 12: Summary / Close

**Content:**
- Headline: Brand name or "Let's Build This Together"
- 2-3 tagline options for consideration
- Key takeaway or call-to-action
- Contact info or next steps (optional)

**Speaker Notes:**
"This is the DNA that defines us. Every decision, every piece of communication, every product choice should ladder back to these fundamentals. Questions?"

**Design Direction:**
- Return to the visual style of the title slide
- Taglines as the focal point
- Clean, memorable close

---

## Cross-Skill Invocation

When invoking `document-skills:pptx` to generate this presentation:

1. **Pass the complete Brand DNA document** as context
2. **Specify the slide structure** above as the outline
3. **Include speaker notes** for each slide
4. **Request visual styling** that aligns with the Visual Identity Direction section
5. **Generate as a .pptx file** that the user can download and customize

**Invocation pattern:**
```
Using the completed Brand DNA for [Brand Name], generate a stakeholder presentation following the Brand DNA Presentation Template. Include speaker notes and apply visual styling consistent with the Visual Identity Direction.
```

---

## Customization Options

Users may request variations:
- **Condensed version** (6-8 slides): Combine Vision/Mission, skip Story, condense Do's/Don'ts
- **Investor version**: Add market opportunity slide, emphasize positioning and differentiation
- **Internal team version**: Add more voice examples, expand Do's/Don'ts, include practical applications
- **Partner/agency version**: Expand Visual Direction, add more brand story context

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing the presentation:

- [ ] Every slide has a clear headline and focused content
- [ ] Speaker notes provide context beyond what's on the slide
- [ ] Brand voice is reflected in the copy (not generic presentation language)
- [ ] Visual direction is consistent with Brand DNA guidance
- [ ] Positioning statement is analytically precise, not marketing fluff
- [ ] Values are specific enough to exclude (not generic platitudes)
- [ ] Examples are included where specified (voice, values)
- [ ] The presentation tells a coherent story from problem to solution to future
